# Negatives of the job

<Steps>

- Telling devs that things are not working
- Dealing with crazy timelines
- Avoiding overwork
- Remaining confident and motivated
- Staying on top of new trends in the QA Space

</Steps>

# Tips to get into the QA Space

<Steps>

- Market yourself
- Hone necessary skills - Testers mindset, attention to detail, Being analytical
- Continously learn
- Do not be afraid to go outside of your comfort zone
- Improve your communication skills
- Look up salary ranges for QA

</Steps>

---
# Summary

<Steps>

- QA is actually fun!
- WIth any other field, there are positives and negatives
- Getting into the QA Space is not impossible

</Steps>

# Important Links

[Ministry of Testing](https://www.ministryoftesting.com/) - Aimed towards persons who want to learn more software testing and different aspects of it. However, the content is geared towards mostly North American and European persons.

---

[Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/index.htm) - In case you want to look up quality assurance job salaries and different types of jobs in the quality assurance space. Doesn't show a lot of jobs for the Caribbean and Quality Assurance.

---

[ISTQB](https://www.istqb.org/certification-path-root/why-istqb-certification.html) - The ISTQB certifies testers globally and shows a clear roadmap for persons who are interested in getting into the field of software testing. However, they focuses heavily on the theory and less on the practical side.

---

[Test Automation University](https://testautomationu.applitools.com/) - TestAutomationU teaches persons how to program and how to get started with test automation using different frameworks. The platform itself is free and is updated with content on a semi-regular basis.

---

[FreeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/learn) - For persons looking to learn how to code, this is one of the best places to go to learn to code and they have different certifications to offer persons of all levels.
